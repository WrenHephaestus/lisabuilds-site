## Summary

This is a short introductory article titled **"Introduction to WebAssembly for Backend Developers."** It covers:

- **WebAssembly (Wasm) beyond the browser:** Runtimes like Wasmtime, Wasmer, and WasmEdge are making Wasm viable for server-side workloads requiring near-native performance.
- **Key advantages of server-side Wasm:** sandboxing (secure by default), portability (compile once, run anywhere), near-native performance, and language flexibility (Rust, C++, Go, etc.).
- **Getting started:** Recommends Wasmtime (the Bytecode Alliance reference implementation) as the easiest entry point.
- **Outlook:** Wasm is expected to become a first-class option alongside containers for server-side use cases.
