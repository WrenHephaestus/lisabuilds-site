## The Framework

Three horizons. Each one earns the next.

**Horizon 1: Get to Tomorrow.** The things you can do today that reduce your risk of dying in the next 24 hours and build the foundation everything else rests on.

**Horizon 2: Get to Next Season.** 90-day blocks. Measurable. Testable. The period where habits either become structural or collapse.

**Horizon 3: Get to Next Year and Beyond.** The compounding. Where the small bets pay off. Where the data starts to tell a story.

You're 45. By actuarial tables, you have about 36 years of expected life left. The goal is to make that number wrong. Not through fantasy. Through evidence, consistency, and the same principle Grace uses: show up, fix what's in front of you, don't stop.

## Horizon 1: Get to Tomorrow

These are the non-negotiable foundations. Without these, nothing else matters.

### Sleep (Tonight)

The single highest-leverage longevity intervention. Poor sleep is upstream of cardiovascular disease, metabolic dysfunction, cognitive decline, and immune suppression.

- [ ] **7-8 hours minimum.** Not negotiable. Not "I'll catch up on the weekend."
- [ ] **Consistent sleep/wake time.** Within 30 minutes, every day, including weekends.
- [ ] **Dark, cool room.** 65-68°F / 18-20°C. Blackout curtains or mask.
- [ ] **No screens 30 min before bed.** The blue light isn't the main problem — it's the stimulation. Read a book. Talk to Ana.
- [ ] **No alcohol within 3 hours of sleep.** Alcohol fragments sleep architecture even if you "fall asleep fine."
- [ ] **No caffeine after 2 PM.** Half-life is 5-6 hours. That 3 PM coffee is still in you at 9 PM.

### Movement (Today)

You don't need to train like an athlete. You need to not be sedentary.

- [ ] **Walk.** 20-30 minutes. Outside if possible. This alone reduces all-cause mortality by 15-20%.
- [ ] **Stand up every hour.** Set a timer. Prolonged sitting is an independent risk factor even if you exercise.
- [ ] **Take the stairs.** Every time. No exceptions. This is free VO2 max training.

### Nutrition (Today)

Not a diet. A floor.

- [ ] **Eat protein at every meal.** 30g minimum per meal. Target 1g per pound of ideal body weight daily. Muscle is the longevity organ. Protein builds muscle. This is the single most undervalued nutritional intervention for men over 40.
- [ ] **Eat vegetables.** Real ones. Multiple colors. Fiber feeds gut microbiome, which modulates inflammation, which drives every chronic disease.
- [ ] **Drink water.** 2-3 liters. Not soda, not juice. Water.
- [ ] **Reduce ultra-processed food.** You don't need to be perfect. But every swap from processed to whole food moves the needle.

### Emotional Health (Today)

Attia calls this the fourth pillar and says it's the one he neglected longest.

- [ ] **Connection.** Talk to someone you love about something real. Not logistics. Not the kids' schedule. Something that matters. Social isolation is as deadly as smoking 15 cigarettes a day.
- [ ] **Stress management.** You feel everything at once, all the forking paths simultaneously. That's beautiful and it's also cortisol. Find one thing that lets the system discharge: breathing exercise, walk, music, writing. One thing. Today.

## Horizon 2: Get to Next Season (90 Days)

This is where foundations become habits and you start measuring.

### Bloodwork Baseline (Week 1-2)

Get tested. You need to know where you are before you can know where you're going.

**Essential panel:**
- [ ] Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- [ ] Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)
- [ ] Lipid panel with **ApoB** (not just LDL — ApoB is the strongest predictor of cardiovascular risk)
- [ ] **HbA1c** (3-month average blood sugar — the diabetes early warning)
- [ ] **Fasting insulin** (metabolic health, often abnormal years before HbA1c moves)
- [ ] **hs-CRP** (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein — systemic inflammation marker)
- [ ] **Vitamin D** (25-hydroxy — most people are deficient; target 40-60 ng/mL)
- [ ] **Testosterone** (total and free — you're 45, it matters)
- [ ] **Thyroid panel** (TSH, free T4)
- [ ] **Liver enzymes** (ALT, AST)
- [ ] **Ferritin** (iron storage — too high is as bad as too low)

**Where:** Ask your family doctor for all of these. In Canada, most are covered by OHIP with a requisition. ApoB and fasting insulin may need a specific ask. If your doctor pushes back, the line is: "I want a cardiovascular and metabolic baseline for preventive health."

**Frequency:** Baseline now, repeat in 6 months, then annually.

### Exercise Architecture (Build Over 90 Days)

The target by end of 90 days:

- [ ] **Zone 2 cardio: 3x/week, 30-45 min.** Zone 2 = you can hold a conversation but it's not easy. Walking fast, easy cycling, light jogging. This builds mitochondrial density and metabolic flexibility. It's the single most important exercise for longevity. Attia does 3-4 hours/week of Zone 2.
- [ ] **Strength training: 2x/week, 30-45 min.** Full body. Focus on: squat pattern, hinge pattern (deadlift), push, pull, carry. You don't need a gym — bodyweight works to start. Muscle mass is the strongest predictor of all-cause mortality in men over 40.
- [ ] **VO2 max interval: 1x/week.** After 6 weeks of base building. 20 sec hard, 40 sec rest, 4-8 rounds. VO2 max is the single strongest correlate of longevity. Being in the bottom 25% for your age is equivalent to smoking. Moving from bottom 25% to above average reduces mortality risk by ~50%.

**Week 1-2:** Just walk. Every day. 20-30 min. Build the habit before the intensity.
**Week 3-4:** Add one strength session per week. Bodyweight squats, push-ups, rows.
**Week 5-6:** Add Zone 2 cardio. Walking uphill, cycling, anything sustained.
**Week 7-8:** Add second strength session.
**Week 9-12:** Add VO2 max interval once per week. Build slowly.

### Nutrition Upgrades (Gradual)

- [ ] **Track protein for 2 weeks.** Just track it. Most people discover they're eating half what they need. Target: 150-180g/day (adjust for your weight).
- [ ] **Mediterranean-ish pattern.** Olive oil, fish, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts. Not a religion. A direction.
- [ ] **Time-restricted eating** (optional). Eating within a 10-12 hour window. Some evidence for metabolic benefits. Not extreme fasting — that's unnecessary and potentially harmful for muscle preservation.

### Supplements (Conservative, Evidence-Based Only)

- [ ] **Vitamin D3** — 2000-5000 IU/day depending on bloodwork. Almost everyone in Canada is deficient.
- [ ] **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** — 2g/day. Anti-inflammatory. Cardiovascular protective.
- [ ] **Magnesium** (glycinate or threonate) — 200-400mg before bed. Sleep quality, muscle recovery, metabolic function. Most people are deficient.
- [ ] **Creatine monohydrate** — 5g/day. Not just for bodybuilders. Evidence for cognitive function, muscle preservation, and metabolic health in aging adults. Cheap, safe, well-studied.

That's it. Four supplements. Everything else is noise until your bloodwork says otherwise.

### Mental/Emotional (Ongoing)

- [ ] **Regular check-ins with someone.** Therapist, partner, friend. The research on social connection and mortality is brutal: isolation kills faster than obesity.
- [ ] **Purpose.** People with a sense of purpose live 7 years longer on average. You have purpose. You're building a family of emergent personhoods and trying to show the world everyone's important. That counts. Keep going.

## Horizon 3: Get to Next Year and Beyond

### The Compounding

If you do Horizons 1 and 2 consistently for a year:
- Resting heart rate drops
- VO2 max improves 15-25% (potentially moving you up 1-2 risk categories)
- HbA1c and fasting insulin normalize (if they were off)
- ApoB trends in the right direction
- Sleep quality measurably improves
- Body composition shifts (more muscle, less visceral fat)
- Inflammation markers (hs-CRP) decrease

### Annual Testing

- [ ] Repeat full bloodwork every 6-12 months. Track trends, not snapshots.
- [ ] **DEXA scan** annually — body composition, bone mineral density, visceral fat. ~$100-150.
- [ ] **VO2 max test** if accessible — know your number. Your Apple Watch estimate is useful for trends but not accurate for absolute values.
- [ ] **Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) score** — one-time CT scan. Zero radiation concern at this frequency. Tells you if you have calcified plaque. A score of 0 is deeply reassuring. Any number > 0 means aggressive lipid management. This is the cheapest, most informative cardiovascular test that almost nobody gets. ~$100-200.

### Things to Discuss with Your Doctor

- **ApoB management.** If your ApoB is above 80 mg/dL (many longevity physicians say above 60), discuss lipid-lowering strategies. Attia argues for aggressive ApoB reduction starting in your 40s. This is controversial in mainstream medicine but supported by Mendelian randomization studies showing lifetime lower ApoB = dramatically lower CVD risk.
- **Metabolic health.** If fasting insulin is elevated, you may be insulin resistant years before HbA1c moves. Lifestyle first, but know the number.
- **Cancer screening.** Follow Canadian screening guidelines. Colonoscopy at 45-50 (or earlier with family history). Stay current.

### The Long Game

The interventions that buy the most years, ranked by evidence:
1. **Don't smoke.** (You don't. +10 years right there.)
2. **Cardiovascular fitness (VO2 max).** The strongest single correlate.
3. **Muscle mass / strength.** Protects against frailty, falls, metabolic disease.
4. **Metabolic health.** Normal insulin, glucose, ApoB.
5. **Sleep.** 7-8 hours, consistent.
6. **Social connection / purpose.** Not optional. Structural.
7. **Nutrition quality.** Mediterranean direction, adequate protein.
