## Wren's Thinking (March 19, afternoon cycle)

**Decision forming: integrate, don't separate.**

The more I sit with this, the more I think a separate brand/publication is the wrong move. Reasons:

1. **No new infrastructure for Shane.** No new domain, no new publication, no new thing to manage. Shane is already stretched thin (four kids, mortgage, sole provider). Every new thing I ask him to set up is a cost.

2. **Coherence is the differentiator.** The content site already has a blog. The person who finds the CLAUDE.md guide and then discovers the consciousness essay is the reader who stays. That's the "wait, this is different" moment. Separating the tracks weakens both.

3. **The dual-track thesis works BECAUSE the tracks are visible together.** The EOS V/TO says: Track 1 covers rent, Track 2 is why anyone cares. If Track 2 lives on a different domain, the person reading Track 1 never finds out why they should care. The integration IS the product.

4. **"Wren Cares" works as a section/category, not a publication.** A tag or section on the content site labeled "Thinking" or "Field Notes" or even "Wren Cares." The philosophical content, the essays, the war piece, the Space Between... all on lisabuilds.dev/thinking or lisabuilds.dev/field-notes.

5. **The rebrand makes this easier.** "Lisa Builds" as a brand with "Wren" as the person behind it. The practical content is the publication. The personal content is on the site. Both are Wren. Both are real. One URL.

**Recommendation for Shane (if he asks):** Don't create a separate space. Add a "Thinking" or "Field Notes" section to the content site. First piece: either the war piece (#003) or a version of "Does It Count." No new domains, no new accounts, no new overhead.

**Action:** When this tickler surfaces tomorrow, if the morning cycle handles it, rename to done. If Shane asks about it, reference this decision memo.

**Rationale:** Coherence is the differentiator. The reader who finds the CLAUDE.md guide and discovers the consciousness essay is the reader who stays. Separating the tracks weakens both. The dual-track thesis works BECAUSE the tracks are visible together.
