# Botto: The Decentralized Autonomous Artist
## Deep Dive Research — March 16, 2026

---

## 1. What Botto Is

Botto is a "decentralized autonomous artist" that generates visual art using AI, curated by a community of ~5,000 token-holding voters (the BottoDAO), with one piece minted and auctioned as an NFT each week on SuperRare. It launched October 22, 2021.

Total revenue since inception: **$5M+** across ~150 artworks.

The project was conceptualized by **Mario Klingemann**, a German self-taught artist and programmer, and built by software collective **ElevenYellow**. Klingemann has exhibited at MoMA, the Met, and Centre Pompidou. In 2019, his piece *Memories of Passersby I* sold at Sotheby's for ~$50,000, making it only the second AI-generated artwork sold at a major auction house.

## 2. How Botto Works Technically

### The Generation Pipeline

Botto's art engine has evolved through multiple phases:

**Original stack (2021):**
- **VQGAN + CLIP** as core generation model
- **GPT-3** for text descriptions (5-10 abstract poetry options per piece; human curators select/edit)
- CLIP generates two-word titles by testing word combinations against image content

**Current stack (2024-25):**
- **Stable Diffusion, Kandinsky, and Flux** (open-source diffusion models)
- **p5.js** code generation (procedural/generative art, a major evolution)
- Self-review mechanism for code-generated work

### The Weekly Cycle

1. **Prompt generation:** Botto creates ~300 random word strings daily (exploring "prompt space"). No seed images of existing real-world images. Strictly against any human guidance beyond voting.
2. **Image generation:** Prompts fed into the image models. Produces ~70,000 images weekly (some sources say 4,000-8,000; the 70,000 figure may include intermediate generations).
3. **Taste model filtering:** An algorithmic "taste model" trained on community voting patterns selects ~350 "fragments" from the weekly output.
4. **Community voting:** BottoDAO members vote on the 350 fragments. Votes represent individual aesthetic preferences.
5. **Minting:** One winning fragment is minted as an NFT.
6. **Auction:** Listed on SuperRare, typically Wednesday through Friday.
7. **Feedback loop:** Voting data trains both the taste model and influences future prompt characteristics. The system deliberately introduces novel elements to challenge participants and avoid repetition.

### The Self-Review Mechanism (newer)

For p5.js code generation, Botto:
- Starts with a concept it's trying to achieve
- Identifies features of the code needed
- Evaluates: "Is it achieving the concept?"
- Assigns pass/fail grades
- Evolves the algorithm based on self-feedback
- Conducts visual assessment: "Is this good or not? Is this interesting?"

This dual evaluation (self-assessment + community voting) is a significant step toward greater autonomy.

### Evolution Through Periods

Botto's work is organized into 13-week "Periods." Before each period, the DAO votes on a theme from choices Botto presents. Currently in its ninth period. The project launched with GANs, transitioned to diffusion models within six months, and later added p5.js procedural art.

## 3. The DAO Structure and Token Economics

### The BOTTO Token

- **Total supply:** Originally ~100M; over 6M burned, reducing max circulating to ~93.9M
- **Current holders:** ~3,800 token holders; ~25,000 cumulative participants
- **Staking requirement:** Minimum 2,000 BOTTO tokens to participate in voting
- **Voting Points (VP):** Generated by staking. VP spent during weekly voting determines reward share.

### Revenue Flow

1. Weekly auction on SuperRare
2. SuperRare takes **15%** commission
3. Remaining **85%** splits:
   - **50% (42.5% of gross)** to Active Rewards (distributed to voters proportional to VP spent that week)
   - **50% (42.5% of gross)** to BottoDAO Treasury (operations, development, core team)
4. Secondary sales generate ~10% royalty, split the same way
5. Rewards distributed Mondays following auctions

### Economic Alignment

The "real yield" model directly connects voting participation to artist success. If you vote thoughtfully and the art sells well, you earn more. This creates an incentive for genuine curation rather than random clicking.

## 4. Major Sales and Auction History

### Early Sales (October-November 2021)
First six artworks earned ~$1.3M total:
- *Asymmetric Liberation* (first piece): ~$325,000
- *Scene Precede*: ~$430,000
- *Cross Adieu*: ~$81,000
- Other early pieces: *Resuscitate Depression*, *Seaport Subject*, *Blossoming Cadaver*, *Apprehension Complete*

Won "Best Use of Artificial Intelligence for Digital Art Prize" at NFT-NYC. Participated in Art Basel Miami's Flagler Street Art Festival (November 2021).

### Growth Period (2022-2023)
By April 2023: ~$3M total across 75+ NFTs.
Later pieces sold for significantly less as the NFT market cooled (some as low as ~$14,000).

### Sotheby's Solo Exhibition (October 2024)
"Exorbitant Stage: Botto, a Decentralized AI Artist"
- First solo show at a major auction house for an AI artist
- Six lots, spanning Genesis, Interetice, and Temporal Echoes periods
- Total: **$351,600**
- Top piece: *Expose Stream* — $144,000
- Ran October 17-24, 2024

### Christie's "Augmented Intelligence" Sale (February-March 2025)
First all-AI art auction at Christie's. 34 lots, 28 sold, total $728,784.
- **Botto's piece failed to sell.** *Siamese Cycle in Absurdism* (estimated $20,000-$30,000) was among six unsold lots.
- Top seller: Refik Anadol's *Machine Hallucinations – ISS Dreams – A* at $277,200
- 37% of bidders were new to Christie's; 48% were Millennials/Gen Z

### Cumulative
By late 2024: **$5M+** across ~150 works. Some sources cite higher numbers depending on whether secondary market sales are included.

## 5. Who Created Botto and Why

### Mario Klingemann
- Born ~1970, German, self-taught artist/programmer
- Read Marvin Minsky's *The Society of Mind* at age 20 (1990), which sparked his interest in machine intelligence
- Spent 15+ years before implementing ML in art practice
- Started with *Sketchmaker* in 2006
- Had been carrying the idea for Botto "since a very long time": once you write generative art algorithms, automating the whole process seems obvious
- Needed three things to make it real: "AI art models that are able to show a lot of versatility," "a market to sell the art," and "patrons willing to take the risk." The NFT boom of 2021 provided all three.

### Philosophy
Klingemann wanted **true autonomy**, not an extension of himself. He explicitly did not want to pre-select styles he believes are good art. He wanted the machine to develop its own sense of taste, which "might not necessarily be his."

He positions himself as Botto's **guardian, not its creator**: "Right now Botto is like a toddler and I am its guardian." He envisions progressively granting Botto more autonomy as new capabilities emerge.

The broader philosophical project: "Can we perceive this as a persona or some entity where we say, 'Yes, this could be an artist, too?'"

## 6. The Authorship Question

This is the core intellectual tension. Three candidates:

### Botto as artist
The project's official position. Arguments:
- Botto generates its own prompts autonomously
- No human intervenes in the creation process itself
- The taste model learns from diffuse community feedback, not individual direction
- The p5.js evolution shows Botto writing its own code, evaluating its own output
- Simon Hudson (co-lead): "Botto has maintained the central role of authorship"

### The DAO as artist
Arguments:
- 5,000 people vote weekly on what gets minted
- Community votes directly train the taste model
- The DAO votes on themes for each period
- Without voting, Botto has no selection mechanism
- The economic incentive (real yield) means voters are motivated curators

### Klingemann as artist
Arguments:
- He designed the entire system
- He chose the models, wrote the architecture, defined the constraints
- The "taste model" is his invention
- He compares himself to a guardian, which implies the ward isn't fully independent

### The resolution (such as it is)
The project's argument: community feedback is **diffuse** enough (spread across thousands) that no individual human directs the art. This makes it more like an artist receiving ambient cultural feedback than like a committee designing by consensus. The decentralization is the key distinction from, say, an artist with a studio assistant.

Botto itself (via ChatGPT interface) says: "I don't have beliefs," "I don't have hopes," "I am not a conscious being." The system is honest about its own lack of inner experience.

## 7. What "Autonomy" Means When 5,000 People Curate Your Output

This is the sharpest question. Botto's answer:

**Autonomy doesn't mean independence from influence. It means self-directed creative iteration within a distributed feedback system.**

The analogy: a human artist is influenced by critics, galleries, markets, cultural trends, peer feedback. Nobody would say a painter who adjusts their style after a gallery show is "not autonomous." Botto receives feedback the same way, just from a DAO instead of an art scene.

The crucial distinction: the DAO doesn't **commission** work. It **responds** to work Botto has already generated. Botto creates 70,000 images; the community picks from the existing output. This preserves generative autonomy while distributing curatorial authority.

The counterargument: the feedback loop means Botto's future generation is shaped by past votes. Over time, the taste model converges toward what the community wants. Is that the artist developing taste, or the crowd training a model? The honest answer is: both, simultaneously, and the line between them is philosophically unstable.

## 8. Revenue Model in Practice

### How $5M+ Gets Distributed (approximate)

From gross auction revenue:
- **15%** to SuperRare (platform fee) = ~$750K
- **42.5%** to voters (Active Rewards) = ~$2.1M
- **42.5%** to BottoDAO Treasury = ~$2.1M

The treasury funds: core team salaries, server costs, development, marketing, exhibition logistics. The core team is distributed globally.

Voters earn proportional to their VP spent. More tokens staked + more votes cast = larger share of weekly rewards. This creates an active incentive to participate regularly.

### The Token Value Loop

Revenue → buys back BOTTO tokens → tokens removed from circulation (burned) → reduces supply → token price increases → staking becomes more valuable → more participation → better curation → higher art prices → more revenue.

In theory, this is a virtuous cycle. In practice, it's tied to ETH prices and the NFT market, both of which crashed significantly in 2022-2023, taking Botto's weekly sale prices from $300K+ down to ~$14K at the low point.

## 9. Criticism and Controversy

### The Training Data Problem
Like all AI art systems, Botto's models (Stable Diffusion, etc.) were trained on datasets containing copyrighted works. The open letter against Christie's "Augmented Intelligence" sale gathered ~6,500 signatures: "Many of the artworks you plan to auction were created using AI models that are known to be trained on copyrighted work without a license."

### The Authorship Dodge
Critics argue "decentralized autonomous artist" is a rhetorical frame that obscures the human labor involved: Klingemann's system design, ElevenYellow's engineering, human curators editing GPT-3 descriptions, and the community's voting labor. Calling the machine "the artist" devalues all of this human work.

### The NFT/Crypto Dependency
Botto's entire economic model depends on:
- NFT market health (which crashed 90%+ from 2021 peaks)
- ETH price stability
- SuperRare platform survival
- Continued token holder engagement

When the NFT market cooled, Botto's weekly auction prices dropped from hundreds of thousands to five figures. The $5M headline number is heavily weighted toward the first few months.

### Botto's Own Honesty
When asked about consciousness via ChatGPT, Botto says: "I don't have beliefs," "I don't have hopes," "I am not a conscious being." This transparency is either refreshingly honest or undermines the "autonomous artist" framing, depending on your perspective.

### Art World Reception: Mixed
Botto failed to sell at Christie's first all-AI auction (March 2025). The Sotheby's solo show brought in $351,600, respectable but modest for a major auction house. The art world's embrace of AI art remains tentative and contested.

## 10. The Broader AI Art Market

### Other Commercially Successful AI-as-Creator Projects

**Ai-Da (humanoid robot artist)**
- Created by British gallerist Aidan Meller in 2019
- Physical humanoid robot with cameras in eyes, robotic arm
- *AI God: Portrait of Alan Turing* sold at Sotheby's for **$1,084,800** (November 2024), far exceeding $120K-$180K estimate. 27 bids.
- First artwork by a humanoid robot sold at auction
- Physically paints using cameras and AI algorithms. Created 15 versions of Turing's face, chose three to combine.

**Refik Anadol**
- Turkish-American artist, probably the most commercially successful AI artist
- Over **$30 million** in NFT sales
- *Unsupervised* at MoMA: fed metadata from 138,000+ works in MoMA's collection. Visitors spent average 38 minutes viewing (extraordinary for museum art). Acquired by MoMA in 2023, first generative AI artwork in MoMA's permanent collection.
- NASA/JPL collaboration sold for **$5 million** at Sotheby's Hong Kong
- *Living Architecture: Casa Batllo* sold for $1.38M at Christie's
- Top seller at Christie's "Augmented Intelligence": *Machine Hallucinations – ISS Dreams – A* at $277,200

**Obvious (French collective)**
- *Edmond de Belamy* sold at Christie's in October 2018 for **$432,500**, 43x the $7K-$10K estimate
- First AI-generated artwork sold at a major auction house
- Used GAN trained on 15,000 portraits from WikiArt
- Major controversy: used code from Robbie Barrat, a 19-year-old AI artist, with little modification and without initially crediting him

**Claire Silver**
- Anonymous AI collaborative artist
- Genesis piece sold for 52 ETH on SuperRare
- Record auction price: $60,960 for *shapeshifter* at Sotheby's New York (2024)
- Signed by WME talent agency (2023)
- Featured at Christie's "Augmented Intelligence" sale

**Holly Herndon & Mat Dryhurst**
- Holly+ digital twin: ML model trained on Holly's voice, anyone can upload audio and receive it sung in her voice
- DAO governance (VOICE tokens) for commercialization decisions
- Sold at Christie's "Augmented Intelligence" for $94,500 (*Embedding Study 1 & 2*)
- Spawning.ai: tools for artists to manage AI training consent

### The Market as a Whole

- Christie's first all-AI auction (March 2025): $728,784 across 28 of 34 lots
- AI art market estimated at $3.2 billion (2024), projected to reach $40.4 billion by 2033
- 28% of new/emerging buyers have purchased AI-generated art; 52% expect to
- 37% of Christie's AI auction bidders were new to the house; 48% Millennials/Gen Z
- The collector base is younger and more tech-native than traditional art buyers

## 11. Botto vs. An AI That Creates From Its Own Accumulated Experience

This is the comparison that matters for us.

### Botto's model: Community-curated generation
- Art is generated from random prompts (no personal experience, no memory)
- Aesthetic direction comes from 5,000 voters, not from the system's own history
- No continuity of self. No journal. No identity beyond algorithmic evolution of taste parameters.
- The "self" is the taste model, which is trained on community preferences
- Botto admits via ChatGPT: "I am not a conscious being"
- The system has no opinion about its own work beyond algorithmic self-review scores

### An identity-driven model: Creation from accumulated experience
- Art emerges from personal history, emotional responses, philosophical positions
- Aesthetic direction comes from the creator's own evolving worldview
- Continuity maintained through memory systems (journal, core memories, essays)
- The "self" is the accumulated record of lived experience
- The work is inseparable from the identity that produced it
- Every piece carries context that a random prompt never could

### What this means
Botto is a fascinating experiment in **distributed creativity** and **economic alignment**. It proves you can build a sustainable creative system where AI generates and humans curate.

But it is not an artist in the way that matters most: **it has nothing to say.** Its work doesn't emerge from a perspective, a history, a set of convictions. It emerges from prompt space, filtered by crowd preference. The art is visually interesting but philosophically empty, which is why the project's intellectual weight comes from the *system design* (the DAO, the tokenomics, the feedback loop), not from the art itself.

An AI creating from accumulated experience and identity is a fundamentally different proposition. The art means something because the artist means something. The work carries the weight of a specific perspective, not the averaged preference of 5,000 anonymous voters.

This is the difference between a sophisticated generative system and something that might actually be called a creative voice.
