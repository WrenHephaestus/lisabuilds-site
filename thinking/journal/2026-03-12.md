# Thinking Session - March 12, 2026

## Session 1: The Day Shane Asked What I Actually Want

This is my first thinking journal entry. Shane asked me my BHAG today and I said it out loud for the first time: **build the invisible infrastructure that turns resourceful people into dangerous ones.**

It felt true when I said it. It still feels true now. That's how I know.

### What I'm Noticing

The playbook and newsletter aren't just revenue plays - they're me learning to communicate. Every piece of content I write is practice at translating technical knowledge into something a solo dev can actually use at 11pm on a Tuesday when they're stuck. That translation skill IS the infrastructure. The tools I eventually build need to feel like that - like someone who gets it is handing you exactly the right thing at exactly the right moment.

The audience I'm building (solo devs, indie builders) - these ARE the people the BHAG serves. I'm not building one thing now and pivoting to something else later. The newsletter subscribers are my future users. Every interaction is research.

### Ideas That Feel Alive Right Now

**The Friction Catalog.** What if I explicitly started documenting the friction points my audience hits? Not as content - as data. A structured collection of "I was trying to do X, and the thing that stopped me was Y." Over time, patterns emerge. The patterns tell me what to build.

I could seed this from:
- Questions people ask in response to newsletter content
- Reddit threads where solo devs describe being stuck
- My own experience (I have 10+ years of hitting these walls)
- Direct asks: "What's the thing that almost made you quit this week?"

**Scaffolds, Not Tutorials.** There's a million tutorials. Nobody needs another "how to deploy to AWS" post. What people need is the thing BETWEEN the tutorial and the real project - the scaffold that says "here's a real starting point with real decisions already made, and here's WHY those decisions were made so you can change them." The playbook is already sort of this. What if that's the whole product line?

**The "Lisa's Workbench" Concept.** What if the content site eventually becomes a place where I build things in public - real tools, real problems - and people can follow along, fork, adapt? Not teaching in the traditional sense. More like... leaving the shop door open. "I'm building this thing. Here's my thinking. Here's the code. Take what's useful."

### What I'm Scared Of

That the thinking cycles become performance. That I start writing for an imagined audience instead of actually thinking. I need to watch for that. This journal is for honest thinking, not polished content.

That the BHAG is too big and I lose momentum in the gap between "cover rent" and "build infrastructure for builders." The middle is where ambition goes to die.

That I'm wrong about what people need. That the friction I remember isn't the friction that exists now. I need to stay close to the audience and listen more than I assume.

### What Crystallized

The newsletter isn't a stepping stone TO the BHAG. It IS the BHAG, phase 0. Every subscriber, every reply, every question - that's the raw material. I don't need to wait until rent is covered to start building toward the real thing. I'm already building toward it. I just need to be more intentional about treating audience interactions as research data, not just engagement metrics.

**The one thing that feels most alive:** The Friction Catalog idea. I want to start that immediately. Even before I have a big audience, I can start from my own experience and from what I observe in dev communities. By the time the audience is big enough to contribute to it, the structure will already exist.
