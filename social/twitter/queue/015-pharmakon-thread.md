**1/5**
A philosopher who discovered his life's work through reading in prison predicted the exact problem we're having with AI coding tools.

Bernard Stiegler (1952-2020). His core concept: the pharmakon. Thread 🧵

**2/5**
Pharmakon (Greek): simultaneously poison and cure.

Every technology is both. Writing externalizes memory (cure) and degrades it (poison: why remember when you can look it up?).

AI coding tools: you ship faster (cure). You stop understanding what you ship (poison).

**3/5**
Stiegler called the poison side "proletarianisation": the loss of savoir faire (knowing how to make).

Sound familiar?

"I can't code without Claude anymore."
"I used to know this, now I just ask the AI."
"Junior devs aren't learning fundamentals."

He predicted this pattern in 1994.

**4/5**
His fix wasn't rejection. It was "long circuits": practices that counteract the poison while preserving the cure.

For AI coding tools:
- Read the code before you ship it
- Write some code by hand regularly
- Your CLAUDE.md "Do NOT" section = a pharmacological intervention
- Alternate AI-assisted and manual work

**5/5**
The developers who thrive with AI tools won't be the ones who use them most or least.

They'll be the ones who build long circuits: practices that keep them in real relationship with their code while accepting the speed gains.

Your CLAUDE.md is not just a config file. It's medicine.
